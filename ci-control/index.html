The Problem

CI wasn't designed for the volume AI brings.

Every AI coding assistant is pushing commits. Your CI is louder, your review queue is deeper, and nobody knows which failures actually matter.

🔊

Too many failures, too little signal

AI tools generate PRs at 5–10× the rate of human developers. Your CI dashboards are a firehose of red — and most of it is flaky tests, not real breakage.

⏳

Reviewers burn hours triaging

Senior engineers spend 30–40% of review time figuring out whether a failure is real, environment-related, or introduced by generated code they didn't review in the first place.

🤖

AI-authored PRs slip through

When Copilot or Cursor writes the code, who's accountable? Without ownership signals and automated routing, generated PRs land on the wrong desk or get rubber-stamped.

Features

Everything you need to trust your CI again.

Built for teams that ship fast and won't accept a noisy pipeline.

Classification

Failure classification engine

Every failed CI run is classified as genuine, flaky, environment, merge-conflict, or AI-originated — using diff analysis, log pattern matching, and historical flake data.

Detection

Flake detection & quarantine

Automatically identifies flaky tests using cross-run correlation, marks them in your test suite, and generates a quarantine report so you can fix them on your schedule.

Routing

AI-authored PR routing

Detects PRs generated by Copilot, Cursor, Codex, and other tools. Routes them to the appropriate code owner with a completeness score and risk summary.

Ownership

Ownership-aware review assignment

Uses CODEOWNERS, git blame history, and dependency graphs to assign reviews to the right person — not just whoever's turn it is on rotation.

Integration

GitHub & GitLab native

Deep integration with GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, CircleCI, and Buildkite. We read your logs, checks, and statuses — no webhook gymnastics required.

Insight

Team health dashboards

Track flake rate, mean time to resolution, review queue depth, and AI-PR volume per team. Make the case for tooling investment with real numbers.
